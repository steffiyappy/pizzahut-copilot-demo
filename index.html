Exercise 1 · Market Intelligence10 min · Researcher · Premium ⭐

When to do this: Right after the pitch. Open M365 Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com and click Researcher.

Use Researcher (Critique mode — the default) to scan quick-service restaurant in Indonesia, benchmark peers, and surface the moves a GM cannot afford to miss this quarter.

Task 1.15 min🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Scan the Quick-Service Restaurant landscape in Indonesia

In M365 Copilot, open Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (this is Critique — dual-model with self-review). Paste this prompt:

Research the Quick-Service Restaurant sector in Indonesia. Benchmark these companies: Pizza Hut Indonesia, Domino's Pizza ID, Pizza Marzano, McDonald's ID, KFC Indonesia, Burger King ID. For each, compare market share, revenue growth FY25, EBITDA margin, strategic focus, digital maturity, and recent material moves (M&A, capex, new contracts, regulatory). Include market sizing 2024–2028 with the top three growth drivers. Cite every source. Flag where evidence is thin.
Notice the citations and the "reviewed by" stamp — Critique enforces source grounding before the report is shown.
Task 1.25 min📝 Word Copilot · 1-page brief

Turn the research into a 1-page GM brief

In the Researcher report, click Export → Convert to Word. Open the file from OneDrive. In the Word Copilot pane, paste:

Based on the content in this document, draft a 1-page GM brief for the General Manager · Operations & Marketing at Contoso Pizza Co. Structure: - Market headline (2 sentences, 1 number) - Top 3 competitive moves we must watch - Top 3 implications for Contoso Pizza Co in the next 90 days - 1 question to put to the Executive Committee Tone: direct, board-ready, no jargon. Max 350 words. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not confirmed in the research. End with assumptions + open questions.
Basic-license fallback: if you do not have Researcher, use M365 Copilot Chat (Web grounding ON) with the same prompt. You will get a competent brief — just without the second-model critique pass.

Create a self-contained one-page HTML infographic on the future of QSR & delivery economics in Indonesia (2026–2028) for Contoso Pizza Co (Quick-Service Restaurant). Audience: General Manager · Operations & Marketing and direct reports. Exercise 3 · PowerPoint — Draft & Digest15 min · PowerPoint Copilot · Basic

When to do this: Open a blank PowerPoint (powerpoint.com or desktop). Make sure 05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx are in your OneDrive.

3.1 · Draft a BCG/McKinsey-style strategy deck

Copilot in PowerPoint will draft a deck in the "one message per slide" style consulting firms use — grounded on your memo and KPI workbook.

Task 3.1a7 min🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

Draft an 8-slide GM strategy deck

In the blank deck, open the Copilot pane. Type / to reference your two files (05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx), then paste:

Create an 8-slide FY26 Outlet Performance & Lebaran Campaign Strategy deck for Contoso Pizza Co, audience: General Manager · Operations & Marketing and Operating Committee. Ground every slide on /05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and the KPI tables in /02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. Use a "one message per slide" McKinsey/BCG style: every slide has (a) a complete-sentence action title that IS the conclusion, (b) supporting evidence in 3 bullets or a small table, (c) one source caption at the bottom. Slide structure: 1. Title — Contoso Pizza Co · FY26 Outlet Performance & Lebaran Campaign Strategy 2. Executive snapshot — headline + 3-number callout + RAG status 3. "Where we are today" — KPI scoreboard table (Same-Store Sales Growth %, Avg Ticket (IDR k), Delivery Mix %, Food Cost %) 4. "What's changing" — top 3 market forces 5. "Three priorities for FY26 H2" — one bullet each, with owner 6. "Where to invest first" — 90-day moves with cost / benefit 7. "Risks & mitigations" — table: risk, owner, status 8. "The ask" — one-page decision summary for the Operating Committee Tone: direct, evidence-led, no adjectives. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for any number not in the source files.
Task 3.1b3 min🎨 Self-critique

Tighten the deck — McKinsey-style critique pass

Act as a McKinsey engagement manager reviewing this deck. For each slide, suggest a sharper action-title (a complete sentence that IS the conclusion). Flag any slide where the title and body do not agree. Suggest one slide we can remove. Reply as a 1-page review note, then apply your edits to the deck.
Why action titles matter: in a BCG/McKinsey deck the slide title is the conclusion ("Same-store sales drag is concentrated in mid-tier department stores"), not the topic ("Same-store sales"). The body just proves it.

3.2 · Digest a deck someone just sent the GM

A 60-slide deck lands in your inbox 20 minutes before the meeting. Copilot reads it for you.

Task 3.25 min🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

Summarise & interrogate an incoming deck

Open any presentation you've received recently (or use the deck you just drafted). In the Copilot pane:

Summarise this presentation in 5 bullet points. What is the single key decision being asked of the audience? What are the 3 numbers I should remember? Identify any action items, owners, and dates. List the top 3 questions a General Manager · Operations & Marketing should ask in the meeting.
Direct from Microsoft's "Copilot for Business Leaders" page — this is the #1 PowerPoint use case for executives: turning incoming decks into a 60-second pre-read.

Exercise 4 · Excel Power Hour20 min · Copilot in Excel · Basic + Premium ⭐

The most-asked GM question: "Show me what's actually happening in the data — without me writing a formula." Excel Copilot now does this in four flavours. We will run all four on 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx.

4.1 · Get the insights — one prompt

Task 4.15 min📊 Excel Copilot · Chat

Ask Copilot to find what matters in 12 months of KPIs

Open 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx from OneDrive. Open the Copilot pane (Home → Copilot). On the Monthly_KPIs tab, paste:

Analyse the Monthly_KPIs tab. For each KPI (Same-Store Sales Growth %, Avg Ticket (IDR k), Delivery Mix %, Food Cost %): - Identify the best- and worst-performing location - Spot any sustained downward trend (2+ months) - Flag any single-month outliers worth a phone call Then compare against Targets_vs_Actual. Which KPI has the largest gap to FY26 target, and which location is dragging it the most? Show results as a 2-line executive summary, then a RAG status table by location.

4.2 · "Edit" — surgical, formula-safe changes

Task 4.23 min✏️ Excel Copilot · Edit (NEW)

Make a precise change without rewriting the workbook

In the Copilot pane, switch to Edit mode. Highlight the Status column on the Targets_vs_Actual tab and ask:

In this column, replace the emoji status with a plain-text 1-word status (Green / Amber / Red) and add conditional formatting matching the colours. Do not change any other cell. Show me the diff before applying.
Why GMs love Edit: they trust it. Edit shows a per-cell diff before applying — no surprise rewrites of working formulas.

4.3 · "Plan" — multi-step transformations with preview

Task 4.35 min📋 Excel Copilot · Plan (NEW)

Build an executive view in three steps Copilot proposes first

Switch the Copilot pane to Plan. Paste:

Plan: on a new sheet called "Exec_View", produce a pivoted summary — one row per location, one column per KPI — showing the FY26 H2 average. Add a "Trend" column showing ▲ / ▬ / ▼ vs the FY26 H1 average. Highlight the worst quartile per KPI in red. Add a 2-sentence executive read-out at the top. Show me the plan first. Let me approve. Then run it.
The "Plan" win: Copilot writes its own work order, you tick / un-tick steps, and only then does it touch the workbook.

4.4 · Analyst Agent — what-if & scenario modelling ⭐

Task 4.47 min🤖 Analyst Agent · Premium

Treat the workbook like a junior analyst

Open copilot.microsoft.com → Agents → built-in Analyst. Upload 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. Try:

You are the General Manager · Operations & Marketing's data analyst. Using the Monthly_KPIs sheet, model a scenario: what would full-year performance look like if the bottom-quartile locations on each KPI improved to the median by Q4? Show the year-end position vs FY26 target for each KPI before and after the uplift. Then identify the 3 highest-leverage actions: where a small move improves the group most.
Why Analyst Agent matters: it runs Python-grade analysis in natural language — regression, forecasting, scenario simulation — without anyone touching code. It is the closest thing to a junior analyst on call 24/7.
Basic-license fallback: 4.1, 4.2 and 4.3 work on Basic seats. 4.4 needs Premium — the Basic alternative is the same scenario prompt in Excel Copilot Chat (less interactive, but still gets you there).

Exercise 5 · Executive 5-Minute Moments15 min · Outlook · Teams · Word · Basic

The lightning round. Five things every GM does before lunch — each one is now a single Copilot prompt. Sourced from Microsoft's "Copilot for Business Leaders" page.

5.1 · Outlook — catch up on a long thread

Task 5.13 min📧 Outlook Copilot

"I just got back from a flight — what did I miss on this thread?"

Open any long email thread in Outlook. Click Summary by Copilot at the top, or in the Copilot pane:

Summarise this email thread in 5 bullets. Who is asking what of whom? What decision is the thread converging on? List any open action items, owners and due dates. Flag where I have been mentioned by name and what is being asked of me.

5.2 · Outlook — draft a reply with the right tone

Task 5.23 min📧 Outlook Copilot

Draft an executive reply — short, on-brand, signed off

In the same thread, click Draft with Copilot:

Draft a reply to this thread on behalf of the General Manager · Operations & Marketing of Contoso Pizza Co. Confirm we are on track on the headline ask, push back gently on the unrealistic deadline, and propose a 30-minute call this week. Tone: warm, direct, hormat. 4 short paragraphs maximum, no jargon. Sign off as Bu Lisa Hartono.

5.3 · Teams — recap the meeting you missed

Task 5.33 min💬 Teams Copilot · Intelligent Recap

"What did the Operating Committee actually decide yesterday?"

Open any Teams meeting recap (Calendar → past meeting → Recap tab). Use the Copilot box:

Summarise this meeting for someone who could not attend. Produce: (1) the 3 decisions taken, (2) the 5 open action items with owner + due date, (3) the topic that took most airtime and why, (4) anyone who pushed back strongly and on what, (5) what was promised to me by name.
Why GMs love this: they reclaim the meetings they skip — they get the decisions, the politics, and the asks of them, in 60 seconds.

5.4 · Word — interrogate a long document

Task 5.43 min📝 Word Copilot

"Find me the risk in this 80-page board pack"

Open 05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx (or any long doc). In the Copilot pane:

Read this document end-to-end. Identify: (1) the 3 critical risks, (2) anything inconsistent or contradicted across sections, (3) anything I would expect a General Manager · Operations & Marketing memo to cover that is missing, (4) the 3 questions I should put back to the author. Quote the exact sentence each finding is anchored to.

5.5 · PowerPoint — pre-read in 60 seconds

Task 5.53 min🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

"What is this 60-slide deck actually asking of me?"

Open the deck you generated in Exercise 3 — or any deck a colleague sent you. In the Copilot pane:

Summarise this presentation in 5 bullets. What is the single decision being asked of the audience? What 3 numbers should I remember? List action items with owners. Then list the top 3 questions a General Manager · Operations & Marketing should ask in the room.
The pattern across all five: the GM does not learn a feature — they learn a single sentence: "Copilot, summarise / draft / interrogate this." Every Microsoft 365 surface answers to it.

6.1 · Cowork-driven personalised GM communications

Forget Word's Mailings ribbon — let Cowork do the merge, draft each letter uniquely tuned to that person's performance band, and queue them as Outlook drafts for the GM to review and send.

Task 6.17 min👥 Cowork · multi-step

One prompt → 14 individually-tuned letters → 14 Outlook drafts

In M365 Copilot, open Cowork. Reference both files with /. Paste:

Cowork — run a personalised quarterly outreach for the General Manager · Operations & Marketing of Contoso Pizza Co. Sources: - Recipient list: /03 Contoso_Mail_Merge_Recipients.xlsx (14 rows: First Name, Full Name, Location, Role, Email, Performance Band, Key Highlight) - Brand & tone: /04 Contoso_Brand_Guidelines.docx (use the "Approved GM Communications Template" exactly) For each row in the recipient list: 1. Write a 180-word personal letter using the approved template. Tune the body to the recipient's Performance Band: - "Top Quartile" → recognition-led, ask them to mentor a neighbouring site. - "On Track" → acknowledge + push on one specific stretch goal relevant to General Manager · Operations & Marketing. - "Watch" → direct, supportive, name one specific 30-day fix. 2. Reference the recipient's Key Highlight verbatim, in the second paragraph. 3. Sign off as Bu Lisa Hartono, General Manager · Operations & Marketing, Contoso Pizza Co. 4. Create the email in Outlook as a DRAFT (do not send) addressed to the recipient's Email column. Subject: "[First Name], a quick note on this quarter at [Location]". After all 14 drafts are queued, give me a summary table: Recipient · Location · Band · Subject preview · Draft link. Pause and confirm before sending any of them.
Why Cowork beats classic Mail Merge: classic merge inserts the same body with field substitutions. Cowork writes a different body for each recipient — and stops at "Drafts" so the GM signs off before anything is sent.

Cowork: shortlist + interview pack + offer-letter draft for #1

Cowork — handle CV screening for the open QSR Area Operations Manager (10+ outlets) role at Contoso Pizza Co. Source: SharePoint folder /HR/Recruitment/Contoso Pizza Co QSR Area Operations Manager (10+ outlets) (12 CVs). Do all of the following: 1. Review every CV in the folder. Score each candidate 1-5 against: multi-outlet P&L, food-cost control, labour scheduling, GoFood/GrabFood mix, ServSafe HACCP. 2. Draft (do NOT send) the introductory email to the #1 candidate inviting them to a screening call. Save as Outlook draft. Mark anything unclear in a CV as [VERIFY]. Do not invent qualifications. Pause and confirm before sending any email.
The win: ChatGPT free → 5 file uploads. M365 Copilot Cowork → entire SharePoint folder, fully grounded, scales 12 → 1,200 CVs with the same prompt.

Prompt tips — for every Copilot surface

The GCSE prompt framework

Goal — what I want at the end (a brief, a deck, a dashboard) Context — who it's for, the situation, constraints Source — the file or data I'm pointing to (use / to reference) Expectations — format, length, what to include, what NOT to include Two lines to add to every prompt: "Do not invent data. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not in the source." "End with assumptions made and open questions."
