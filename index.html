Exercise 1 · Market Intelligence10 min · Researcher · Premium ⭐

When to do this: Right after the pitch. Open M365 Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com and click Researcher.

Use Researcher (Critique mode — the default) to scan quick-service restaurant in Indonesia, benchmark peers, and surface the moves a GM cannot afford to miss this quarter.

Task 1.15 min🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Scan the Quick-Service Restaurant landscape in Indonesia

In M365 Copilot, open Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (this is Critique — dual-model with self-review). Paste this prompt:

Research the Quick-Service Restaurant sector in Indonesia. Benchmark these companies: Pizza Hut Indonesia, Domino's Pizza ID, Pizza Marzano, McDonald's ID, KFC Indonesia, Burger King ID. For each, compare market share, revenue growth FY25, EBITDA margin, strategic focus, digital maturity, and recent material moves (M&A, capex, new contracts, regulatory). Include market sizing 2024–2028 with the top three growth drivers. Cite every source. Flag where evidence is thin.
Notice the citations and the "reviewed by" stamp — Critique enforces source grounding before the report is shown.
Task 1.25 min📝 Word Copilot · 1-page brief

Turn the research into a 1-page GM brief

In the Researcher report, click Export → Convert to Word. Open the file from OneDrive. In the Word Copilot pane, paste:

Based on the content in this document, draft a 1-page GM brief for the General Manager · Operations & Marketing at Contoso Pizza Co. Structure: - Market headline (2 sentences, 1 number) - Top 3 competitive moves we must watch - Top 3 implications for Contoso Pizza Co in the next 90 days - 1 question to put to the Executive Committee Tone: direct, board-ready, no jargon. Max 350 words. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not confirmed in the research. End with assumptions + open questions.
Basic-license fallback: if you do not have Researcher, use M365 Copilot Chat (Web grounding ON) with the same prompt. You will get a competent brief — just without the second-model critique pass.

Exercise 2 · Branded Infographic15 min · Copilot Chat — beat ChatGPT

The ask: a GM showed us a ChatGPT infographic last week and said: "Not impressed." Today we fix that. We will get Copilot to draft a branded, on-message HTML infographic — not a generic chart.

Open M365 Copilot Chat at copilot.microsoft.com. Keep the conversation in Chat (not Researcher). Reference your Word brief from Exercise 1 with /.

Task 2.110 min🎨 Copilot Chat · HTML output

Generate a branded one-page HTML infographic

Paste this prompt. The key is the self-contained HTML ask + the explicit visual structure — Copilot will return an HTML file you can preview by saving as .html and opening in a browser.

Create a self-contained one-page HTML infographic on the future of QSR & delivery economics in Indonesia (2026–2028) for Contoso Pizza Co (Quick-Service Restaurant). Audience: General Manager · Operations & Marketing and direct reports. Brand: - Primary colour: #dc2626 - Secondary colour: #f87171 - Font: Inter or Segoe UI, no external CSS imports - Tone: confident, data-led, no buzzwords Required sections (in this order): 1. Hero band — title, sub-title, today's date, our logo placeholder 2. "Market in numbers" — 4 stat tiles (number + 1-line caption) 3. "Three forces shaping our market" — 3 cards with icon, headline, 2-sentence explanation 4. "What this means for Contoso Pizza" — 3 implications + 1 GM action each 5. "90-day moves" — timeline with 3 milestones 6. Footer with sources placeholder Output: a single HTML file with inline CSS only, no JavaScript, mobile-friendly. Use grid layout, generous whitespace, big numbers, and clean dividers. Do not invent statistics — use [PLACEHOLDER] for any number not anchored to a source.
How to view: select the HTML block in Copilot's reply → Copy → paste into Notepad → save as infographic.html → double-click to open in your browser. Screenshot or PDF-print for the GM pack.

Add a hero image

Design a 16:9 hero image for the infographic above: flat illustration, no text on the image, quick-service restaurant themed, using the brand colours #dc2626 and #f87171 as the dominant palette. Professional, executive-tone. Square crop also OK.

Exercise 3 · BCG/McKinsey-style Deck15 min · PowerPoint Copilot · Basic

When to do this: Open a blank PowerPoint (powerpoint.com or desktop). Make sure 05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx are in your OneDrive.

Copilot in PowerPoint will draft a strategy deck in the structured, single-message-per-slide style consulting firms use — grounded on your memo and KPI workbook.

Task 3.110 min🎨 PowerPoint Copilot

Draft an 8-slide GM strategy deck

In the blank deck, open the Copilot pane. Type / to reference your two files (05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx), then paste:

Create an 8-slide FY26 Outlet Performance & Lebaran Campaign Strategy deck for Contoso Pizza Co, audience: General Manager · Operations & Marketing and Operating Committee. Ground every slide on /05 Contoso_Strategic_Memo.docx and the KPI tables in /02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. Use a "one message per slide" McKinsey/BCG style: every slide has (a) a complete-sentence action title that IS the conclusion, (b) supporting evidence in 3 bullets or a small table, (c) one source caption at the bottom. Slide structure: 1. Title — Contoso Pizza Co · FY26 Outlet Performance & Lebaran Campaign Strategy 2. Executive snapshot — headline + 3-number callout + RAG status 3. "Where we are today" — KPI scoreboard table (Same-Store Sales Growth %, Avg Ticket (IDR k), Delivery Mix %, Food Cost %) 4. "What's changing" — top 3 market forces 5. "Three priorities for FY26 H2" — one bullet each, with owner 6. "Where to invest first" — 90-day moves with cost / benefit 7. "Risks & mitigations" — table: risk, owner, status 8. "The ask" — one-page decision summary for the Operating Committee Tone: direct, evidence-led, no adjectives. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for any number not in the source files.
Task 3.25 min🎨 PowerPoint Copilot · refine

Tighten the deck — McKinsey-style critique

After the draft renders, ask Copilot to self-critique:

Act as a McKinsey engagement manager reviewing this deck. For each slide, suggest a sharper action-title (a complete sentence that IS the conclusion). Flag any slide where the title and body do not agree. Suggest one slide we can remove. Reply as a 1-page review note, then apply your edits to the deck.
Why action titles matter: in a BCG/McKinsey deck the slide title is the conclusion ("Same-store sales drag is concentrated in mid-tier department stores"), not the topic ("Same-store sales"). The body just proves it.

4.1 · Personalised GM communications

Drive personalised recognition + direction from one Word letter and one Excel list. The brand-approved template lives in 04 Contoso_Brand_Guidelines.docx.

Task 4.1a5 min📝 Word Copilot · draft template

Draft the GM letter template with Copilot

Open a new Word doc. In the Copilot pane, type / to reference 04 Contoso_Brand_Guidelines.docx, then paste:

Draft a 200-word personal letter from the General Manager · Operations & Marketing of Contoso Pizza Co to a Area & Outlet Manager. The letter must use the "Approved GM Communications Template" in /04 Contoso_Brand_Guidelines.docx exactly — keep the same merge fields in [brackets]: [First Name], [Location], [Key Highlight]. Tone: warm, hormat, direct. Sign off as Bu Lisa Hartono. Keep it as a clean Word template ready for Mail Merge.
Task 4.1b10 min✉️ Word · Mailings tab

Run the mail merge against the Excel recipients

Stay in Word. Use the classic Mailings tab — Copilot prepared the template, Word handles the merge:

  1. Mailings → Start Mail Merge → Letters.
  2. Select Recipients → Use an Existing List — pick 03 Contoso_Mail_Merge_Recipients.xlsx from OneDrive.
  3. Replace each [bracket] with Insert Merge Field (First Name, Location, Key Highlight).
  4. Preview Results — scroll the 14 letters; spot-check 3.
  5. Finish & Merge → Send Email Messages (subject from the template, Email column from the recipient list).
Why Copilot + Mail Merge: Copilot wrote a brand-compliant, GM-toned letter in seconds. Mail Merge does the personalisation at zero cost. The Area & Outlet Managers get a hand-signed-feeling note — every quarter, every site.

4.2 · CV screening at scale (vs ChatGPT's 5-file cap)

The HR team complained: ChatGPT only lets them attach 5 CVs. We have 12 in the SharePoint folder — and in production, hundreds.

Task 4.2a5 min📁 SharePoint setup · one-off

Prep: drop the CVs in SharePoint or OneDrive

  1. Open the samples/CVs/ folder (12 CV_*.docx files).
  2. Upload them to a SharePoint document library — e.g. HR / Recruitment / Contoso Pizza Co QSR Area Operations Manager (10+ outlets) – Apr 2026.
  3. Wait ~2 min for Microsoft Graph to index them.
Task 4.2b10 min🔍 M365 Copilot Chat · folder grounding

Ask Copilot to screen all 12 CVs in one prompt

In M365 Copilot Chat, type / and reference the SharePoint folder (not the individual files). Paste:

Review every CV in this SharePoint folder and shortlist the top 5 candidates for the role of QSR Area Operations Manager (10+ outlets) at Contoso Pizza Co. For each shortlisted candidate, produce: - Name and current employer - 3 evidence bullets pulled directly from their CV - Score 1–5 against each of: multi-outlet P&L, food-cost control, labour scheduling, GoFood/GrabFood mix, ServSafe HACCP - Recommended interview focus areas (2 bullets) - Risks or gaps to test in interview End with: - A ranked shortlist table (top 5) - A "second look" list (next 3) - A reject list with one-line reasoning each Do not invent qualifications. If something is unclear in a CV, mark as [VERIFY].
The win: ChatGPT free → 5 files max. M365 Copilot Chat → entire SharePoint folder, no upload, no number-of-files cap, grounded in your tenant. The same prompt scales from 12 CVs to 1,200.
⭐ Premium bonus10 min🤖 Agent Builder

Wrap the workflow in a reusable HR Screening Agent

Build a custom agent so HR can re-use this every time a role opens — without re-typing the prompt. copilot.microsoft.com → Create → New Agent.

Name: Contoso Pizza HR Screening Agent Description: Reviews CVs in a SharePoint folder and produces a shortlist for any open role at Contoso Pizza Co. Instructions: You are a recruitment screening assistant for Contoso Pizza Co. When the user gives you a SharePoint folder and a target role, evaluate every CV in the folder. Score each candidate on relevant skills, produce a ranked top-5 shortlist, a "second look" list, and a reject list. Always cite the specific CV section your evidence comes from. Never invent qualifications — mark unclear items [VERIFY]. Knowledge: Add the SharePoint folder containing the CVs.

Exercise 5 · Cowork — end with a live HTML dashboard15 min · Cowork · Premium ⭐

The finale. Cowork is Copilot's "agentic" mode — it takes a multi-step outcome and orchestrates the work, checking with you before any action that has consequences (emails, meeting invites, file creation).

We'll hand Copilot one outcome — "wrap the GM's day with a dashboard" — and watch it analyse, draft, schedule, and visualise.

Task 5.110 min👥 Cowork · multi-step

One prompt. Five outputs. One HTML dashboard.

In M365 Copilot, open Cowork. Reference 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx using /. Paste:

Cowork — wrap up this quarter for the General Manager · Operations & Marketing at Contoso Pizza Co. Use /02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx as the source of truth. Do all of the following, in order: 1. Analyse the Monthly_KPIs and Targets_vs_Actual tabs. For each KPI, identify the best-performing location, the worst, and the variance vs the FY26 target. Flag any location in 🔴 status. 2. Draft a 1-page Word document called "Contoso Pizza Q-End GM Summary" covering: top 3 wins, top 3 issues, and a 90-day action plan with owners. Save it to my OneDrive. 3. Draft an executive email from the GM to the Operating Committee with the headline summary and a link to the Word doc. 4. Schedule a 30-minute Operating Committee review for next Wednesday at 10:00. 5. Generate a self-contained HTML KPI dashboard called "Outlet Performance & Delivery Mix Dashboard" with: - Header band in our brand colour #dc2626 - 5 KPI scorecards across the top (one per KPI in Targets_vs_Actual, with the RAG status icon) - A small inline table per location showing last 3 months trend - "Top issue" callout box and "Top action" callout box at the bottom - All inline CSS, no JavaScript, mobile-friendly - Save as Dashboard.html to my OneDrive Pause and confirm with me before sending the email or sending the meeting invite.
What to point out to the audience: Cowork shows its plan first; you can edit it; it pauses before any external action; it returns artifacts (Word, email draft, calendar invite, HTML file) you own.
Task 5.25 min📊 Copilot in Excel · Edit & Plan

(New) Use Copilot in Excel "Edit" and "Plan" — the audience will not have seen these

Open 02 Contoso_KPI_Dashboard.xlsx. In Copilot, switch from Chat to Edit (single-cell precise edits) and Plan (multi-step transformations Copilot proposes before applying):

Plan: take the Monthly_KPIs tab and produce, on a new sheet called "Exec_View", a pivoted summary with one row per location and one column per KPI showing the FY26 H2 average. Add conditional formatting so the worst quartile per KPI is highlighted red. Show me the plan, let me approve, then run it.
Why this lands with GMs: Edit gives them surgical changes (no broken formulas), Plan gives them visibility (no surprise rewrites). It is the missing UX layer that turned Excel Copilot from "demo magic" into "I trust it on my workbook".
Basic-license fallback: if Cowork is not available, the same finale lands as a 3-prompt chain in Copilot Chat (analyse → draft email → generate HTML). Slower and you orchestrate, but the artifacts are the same.

Prompt tips — for every Copilot surface

The GCSE prompt framework

Goal — what I want at the end (a brief, a deck, a dashboard) Context — who it's for, the situation, constraints Source — the file or data I'm pointing to (use / to reference) Expectations — format, length, what to include, what NOT to include Two lines to add to every prompt: "Do not invent data. Use [PLACEHOLDER] for anything not in the source." "End with assumptions made and open questions."

Contoso Pizza-ready reusable prompts

Researcher · Sector scan
Research [topic] in the quick-service restaurant sector in Indonesia. Compare [3-4 peers]. Cite all sources. Flag where evidence is thin.
Excel · KPI table
Analyse [tab name] in this workbook. For each location calculate [metric]. Flag the bottom quartile in red. Add a 2-sentence executive read-out at the top.
Word · GM brief
Draft a 1-page brief for the General Manager · Operations & Marketing. Cover: headline, three findings, three actions, ask of the committee. Max 350 words. Do not invent data.
PowerPoint · BCG-style
Draft an 8-slide strategy deck on [topic]. One message per slide. Action titles (complete sentences). Body proves the title in 3 bullets. Source caption at the bottom of every slide.
Cowork · delegation
Cowork — analyse [source file], draft [Word output], draft [email], schedule [meeting], generate [HTML deliverable]. Pause and confirm before sending anything external.
